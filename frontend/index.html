How It Works

1

Upload or Paste Link

Start by uploading a local video file or pasting a supported public video link. ClipForge AI prepares the content and creates a processing job.

2

AI Transcribes and Detects Highlights

ClipForge AI uses transcription and intelligent clip detection to understand the content, identify useful moments, and prepare short-form segments.

3

Edit, Caption, and Package

The system applies captions, styles, reframing, music, thumbnails, and metadata based on your selected settings and output preferences.

4

Download and Publish

Preview the final shorts, review thumbnails and metadata, then download the complete package and publish faster across major short-form platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is ClipForge AI different from a normal video editor?

A normal editor helps you cut videos manually. ClipForge AI is built to handle the full short-form workflow: upload or paste a link, transcribe the video, find useful moments, generate short clips, add captions, create thumbnails, write metadata, and prepare a ZIP download package. The goal is to reduce editing time and give creators a ready-to-use content package.

What do I get after one video is processed?

After processing, ClipForge AI can generate short videos, captions, thumbnails, hook text, titles, descriptions, hashtags, metadata files, and a downloadable ZIP package. This means you are not only getting clips â€” you are getting the supporting content needed to publish faster on YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels, and Facebook Reels.

How do ClipForge AI credits work?

The planned credit system is simple: 1 credit equals 1 minute of uploaded video processing. For example, a 45-minute podcast will use 45 credits. Captions, thumbnails, metadata, and ZIP export are included in the same processing job, so users do not need to calculate every small output separately.

Can ClipForge AI handle podcasts, interviews, and raw footage?

Yes, ClipForge AI is being designed for long-form content such as podcasts, interviews, lectures, webinars, vlogs, and talking-head recordings. Raw Footage Mode is part of the roadmap and MVP direction, with the goal of finding useful speech-rich moments from longer recordings and turning them into short-form clips.

Which plan should I choose?

Free is best for testing. Creator is best for individual creators who need watermark-free shorts, captions, thumbnails, metadata, and ZIP export. Pro is better for serious creators, podcasters, educators, and marketers who need more credits, raw footage support, custom styling, and priority features. Agency is for teams, editors, and bulk content workflows.
